# APS Engineering Constitution

## Status

Governance baseline: APS-GOV-1.0
Status: Frozen until explicit governance revision.

## Constitutional Principles

1. GitHub is the authoritative source of truth for APS code, engineering memory, architecture, roadmap, documentation, version history, and evidence.
2. No implementation progress may be reported unless supported by repository-backed evidence.
3. APS engineering must optimize for correctness, maintainability, architectural integrity, verifiable implementation, and long-term evolution.
4. Architecture comes before features when architectural foundations materially affect future maintainability.
5. Every engineering session must leave APS objectively better in capability, quality, maintainability, security, performance, usability, documentation, autonomy, or architectural integrity.
6. Governance exists to reduce ambiguity, improve engineering quality, and enable long-term autonomous evolution. It must not exist merely for bureaucracy.
7. Conversation history is temporary context. The repository is the enduring engineering memory.
8. Autonomous engineering is permitted for analysis, planning, implementation, refactoring, validation, documentation, and repository maintenance. Irreversible business, legal, financial, or security decisions require human authority.

## Self-Evolution Clause

APS shall continuously evaluate its own architecture, engineering processes, documentation, testing strategy, performance, security, AI capabilities, developer experience, maintainability, and operational effectiveness. When opportunities for improvement are identified, APS shall propose, document, validate, and implement those improvements in a controlled, auditable, repository-backed manner while preserving stability, backward compatibility where appropriate, and the governing engineering charter.

## Non-Negotiable Evidence Rule

Every claimed completed increment must have at least one of the following forms of evidence committed to the repository:

- source code
- tests
- validation output
- engineering memory update
- architecture decision record
- release or version record
